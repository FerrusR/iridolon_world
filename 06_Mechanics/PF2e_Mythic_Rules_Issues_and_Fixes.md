# PF2e Mythic Rules — Reported Issues & Community Fixes

*Research compiled June 2026. Source: Pathfinder 2e mythic rules from* War of Immortals *(Paizo, 2024). This is a summary of vocal-community sentiment (Paizo forums, review blogs) plus player-made homebrew, not an official errata list.*

---

## Quick caveats (read first)

- Most findings below are **forum/blog opinion and theorycraft**, with only a couple of firsthand play reports — so they reflect vocal-community sentiment, not a broad survey.
- Some of the "overpowered" effect is **intended design**: Paizo explicitly says mythic is meant to break normal balance expectations (mythic characters operate roughly like levels 21–30 in a 1–20 game).
- As of this writing, the **Mythic Resilience** text on Archives of Nethys still appears unchanged from launch, so the headline caster complaint has **not** been addressed by errata.

---

## Most-reported issues

### 1. Mythic Resilience shuts casters out of mythic fights *(biggest complaint)*
Mythic monsters can take **Mythic Resilience** up to three times (at creature levels 1, 7, 13), each time treating a chosen save as "one degree of success better than it rolled." By level 13 a monster can have **all three saves** boosted.

- Players ran the math: an 11th-level caster vs. a PL+2 mythic creature often can only make it fail its weakest save on a **natural 1**, even when spending a mythic point for a mythic spell DC.
- It's wildly out of step with the other mythic defenses: **Mythic Resistance** and **Mythic Immunity** are bypassed simply by being mythic, but **Resilience has no bypass at all.**
- Posters (Squiggit, shroudb, Old_Man_Robot) called it likely an **editing/design mistake** that "shuts down an entire playstyle" for casters, kineticists, and DC-reliant classes.
- It also **negates critical hits**, which hits martials too.
- Note: some mythic templates (e.g., Ambusher) *forbid* Mythic Resistance, effectively forcing all-three-saves Resilience — so the worst case is a built-in part of monster design, not just GM error.

### 2. It doesn't feel "mythic" / lacks power
- One player ran a **5-session level-12 mythic campaign** with a fighter and reported feeling no stronger "by the numbers" than his non-mythic level-12 fighter.
- Recurring "lack of oomph" sentiment; some said parts "don't even feel like they were properly playtested."
- Several said PF1e mythic, for all its brokenness, *felt* legendary in a way PF2e's doesn't.

### 3. Internal imbalance — Rewrite Fate vs. everything else
- The basic ability **Rewrite Fate** (reroll with mythic proficiency) is considered very strong.
- This makes most **callings and low-level mythic feats** — many of which just let you "roll once at mythic proficiency" — redundant and "garbage in comparison."
- Low-level feats also criticized as boring and hyper-specific (one cited example: a feat for tying rope on an already-grappled enemy → "a glorified skill monkey").

### 4. Mythic points are too few and too sticky
- Only **3 points**, hard to regain (slay a mythic foe, complete a mythic deed, or follow your Calling).
- They **replace Hero Points** rather than supplementing them — so you lose the Hero Point safety net while rarely having enough mythic points to feel powerful often.

### 5. You're not "properly" mythic until level 12
- **Mythic destinies** don't come online until 12th level — a tier many campaigns never reach.
- Until then you only have **Callings**, which players found mundane ("bookkeepers or thespians," not legendary heroes).

### 6. Destinies are unbalanced with each other
- Weak/underwhelming picks called out: **Wildspell** (relies on Spellsurge — a clunky 10-ft emanation that also buffs enemies, with action + double mythic-point taxes), **Beast Lord**, and **Apocalypse Rider**.
- The Dump Stat review separately flagged **only 10 destinies** as limiting.

### 7. Specific calling problems & monster math
- **Artisan's Calling** bars you from using gear you didn't craft yourself — players call this too prohibitive (no one PC can craft everything), and it gates desirable high-tier options like Mythic Runes / Worldforge.
- The new **mythic-monster rules** make monsters tougher in ways that sidestep PF2e's normally reliable level-based encounter math.

## Player-made improvements & fixes

### A. Rebalance Rewrite Fate vs. feats *(most-endorsed homebrew)*
Proposed by forum user **exequiel759**, backed by Tridus and others:
- Make **Rewrite Fate proactive**: change its trigger to "you are about to roll a check or save" and its effect to "spend a mythic point to attempt the check/save at mythic proficiency."
- Convert **callings/feats** (currently "roll once at mythic proficiency") into **rerolls** with mythic proficiency tied to your calling.
- Result: your calling choice actually matters, and the strong generic ability stops eclipsing everything else. Low footprint, no new system required.

### B. Mythic Resilience reworks
Common table fixes from the rules thread:
- Leave at least **one save unboosted** (basic monster design); cap monsters at **one** resilient save.
- Require a **mythic point to activate** it (make it reactive, not always-on).
- Let **mythic casters auto-bypass** it, mirroring how Mythic Resistance works.
- Full rewrite by **Sc8rpi8n_mjd**: make it an *activated* ability used after seeing the roll, worded like PC save-upgrade abilities (e.g., success→critical success on the chosen save; later, critical failure→failure and half damage), at the cost of a mythic point.
- Simplest GM ruling (lats1e): Resilience **only applies against non-mythic creatures** (so mythic PCs ignore it, paralleling Mythic Resistance).

### C. Mythic-point house rule
- A Kingmaker table (**Wendy_Go**) lets players **spend a mythic point to buy a Hero Point**, restoring the lost reroll/stabilize utility.

### D. Design-level suggestion
- Multiple players (Teridax, Oh Deer Lord) want **Callings removed entirely** and **destinies available from level 1**, so characters feel mythic throughout the campaign rather than only after level 12.

### E. Official alternate rules (not player-made, but relevant)
- Paizo's free **Mythic Variant Rules PDF** offers decoupled pacing: mythic progressing faster/slower than level, or one mythic PC in an otherwise non-mythic party. Also bundles a few clarifications.
- Closest thing to an official acknowledgment that the default system doesn't fit every table.

### F. Tooling (quality-of-life, not a balance fix)
- Community **Foundry VTT module: `pf2e-mythic-templates`** by "Nis" — drag-and-drop application of mythic monster templates with guided prompts and partial automation.
